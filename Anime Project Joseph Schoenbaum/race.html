Black representation in Anime

        Japanese animation has been around for many decades dating back to the 1960s ands 1970s. A lot of ideas 
        Japanese animators got came from American animation back in the 1940s and 1950s. In a lot of early American animation
        black people are painted in a negative manner. To add on top of this, Japan is a homogenous country with 98.5% of its 
        population being Japanese. Due to this, there is extremely poor representation for other races, in particular black people. 
        Still, there is no excuse for not giving black characters a fairer role in their animes.

Exploring the Issue

  • While One piece and Pokemon are decades old, they are still two of the most popular animes of all time. The two characters of Jinx and Mr.Popo show black people in a very racist manner by the way they were illustrated. It shows a lack of understanding of black people from the Japanese animators to draw these characters in a "golliwog-like" manner.

  • Naruto does a great job building out the world throughout the anime. In this world building the audience comes across the hidden cloud village which a village of mainly black people. The main character from this village is Killer Bee. All his lines are rap verses. He rarely speaks when he is not rapping. Again, this shows a lack of understanding by the Japanese animators. All they know of black people is from American culture where they popularized hip hop and rap.

  • In Hunter X Hunter, a black character Canary plays a supporting role. She is the bodygaurd to one of the main character's family never disobeying an order. In the entire show there is always a sense that she is a step below the family, at one point even being knocked uncounscious for failing to live up to the mother of the family's orders. Having a black character in this type of role shows how American culture has impacted anime.

  • Aran Ojiro is the only black supporting character in the anime Haikyu. He is known for his athletic ability and extremely strong spikes, but his weakness is lack of mental fortitude when the other team attacks him. This is another common stereotype of the strong tall black athlete from American culture. 

While I'm not trying to say these stereotypes are an abomination, they definetly show a lack of understanding
        from the Japanese community of black people. Anime needs to do a better job giving black people various types of roles and platforms
        within anime. Shows such as Carole & Tuesday do a great job of this. Anime has a large black community with shows like Boondocks
        being inspired by Japanese animation. To further grow and support this community black anime characters need to have more important
        roles. This video shows a lot of great examples of how anime poorly portrays black people.
